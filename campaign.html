Introduction

Hello, I’m Joshua Chikosha—an ECE Honors student at UT Austin with a passion for bridging hardware and software, building lasting community, and driving real impact. I’m running for NSBE President to unify our chapter, strengthen our corporate ties, and champion every voice—from incoming freshmen to seasoned upperclassmen.

Below you’ll find my expanded vision for how we can create a vibrant, engaged, and supportive NSBE community at UT Austin. Together, we can elevate our chapter to new heights!

Opportunity Initiative & Engagement

One of the biggest hurdles I’ve observed is a lack of applying or taking the initiative to seize the many scholarships, internships, and research openings available. Often, members **underestimate** how far direct outreach, repeated applications, and leveraging our NSBE network can take them. Here’s how I plan to fix that:

1. “Application Accountability” Events
  • Host monthly “Application Power Hours” where members bring laptops & actively apply to internships, REUs, or scholarships **on the spot**
  • Upperclassmen or past internship holders rotate around to help tweak resumes, personal statements, or cover letters
  • I've noticed that sometimes you just have to make people take action or else they won't do it
  • Early in each semester, compile an updated list of national NSBE scholarships, REU deadlines, and other grants
  • During the power hours have people review essays or cover letters for those scholarships
2. Emphasizing NSBE’s Community & Support
  • Dedicate time each GBM to highlight a helpful resource—like a Slack “Homework Help” channel or a “Mock Interview” buddy system
  • Share short personal anecdotes of how the NSBE network helped me & others land roles or solve tough classwork
  • Encourage a culture of reciprocal mentoring—everyone invests in each other’s success
3. Making GBMs More Rewarding
  • Raffles & Mystery Prizes at General Body Meetings (like our sisters orgs do) for small items or gift cards to spark excitement & consistent attendance
  • Spotlight “Opportunity of the Week” at each GBM: a new scholarship, hackathon, or upcoming professor’s lab opening
  • Demonstrate how to do “LinkedIn Warrior” outreach—search for employees at target companies, send personal notes, etc.

Implementation & Accountability

Every goal is only as good as our ability to execute & measure it. Here’s how I’ll keep us on track & transparent:

1. Monthly Updates towards Goals
  • Short presentation each GBM summarizing progress on Hard/Soft Goals
  • Include sponsor updates and the upcoing corporate sponsor for the next GBM
  • Open Q&A for member concerns or new ideas after GBMs with Top 5
2. Semesterly Online Feedback Form
  • An anonymous form at mid-semester to gauge membership satisfaction
  • Collect feedback on events, leadership, or improvement areas
  • Adjust next semester’s approach based on real data & suggestions
3. Officer Accountability
  • Each officer submits a brief “progress report” before E-board meetings
  • Track zone-based deliverables so tasks don’t stall
  • Empower members to see leadership performance & hold them to commitments
  • Fair enforcement: if an officer consistently underperforms, we won’t be afraid to replace them for the org’s benefit
  • A set and enforced strike system in the case of a lack of performance/causing problems
4. CEB Engagement & Meeting Restructure
  • “Talk to 2 new members each GBM” rule: each CEB officer must greet or chat with at least two new or shy members
  • Think Tank Approach: part of each CEB meeting for brainstorming solutions, not just status updates
  • Monthly CEB Socials: off-campus or fun events so the team bonds outside formal tasks
  • Top 5 Weekly Check-Ins: possibly every Friday, a 1-hour sync to share progress & reflect
  • Potentially use Miro boards or rotating group tasks if beneficial
  • President mediates conflicts or underperformance with clarity & fairness
  • Don’t just stick to what’s always been done if we can innovate or pivot for better results
