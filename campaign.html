Introduction

Hello, I’m Joshua Chikosha—an ECE Honors student at UT Austin with a passion for bridging hardware and software, building lasting community, and driving real impact. I’m running for NSBE President to unify our chapter, strengthen our corporate ties, and champion every voice—from incoming freshmen to upperclassmen.

Below you’ll find my expanded vision for how we can create a vibrant, engaged, and supportive NSBE community at UT Austin. Together, we can elevate our chapter to new heights!

Hard Goals

These are top-priority objectives I plan to help deliver as President:

1. Corporate Affairs
  • Secure three new large company sponsorships (or bigger re-signs) by mid-summer
  • Restructure crporate sponsorship packages(which may result in even cheaper packages)
  • Increase the number of lunch and learns occuring each semester
  • Have a company at every single GBM
  • Additionally, a larger quantity of companies would help with member career opportunities
  • Create a data document detailing past corporate and company engagement
  • Dedicated “Sponsor Portal” on the official NSBE website
2. Point System Overhaul
  • Create a real-time online scoreboard to track event attendance & engagement
  • Award top participants with small prizes (chapter merch, gift cards, etc.)
3. Strengthen Faculty Connections
  • Reach out to professors for labs and opportunities that NSBE students could be a part of
  • Invite Black faculty to GBMs, lunch-&-learns, or Q&A panels
  • Collaborate on research/mentorship; show members how approachable they are
  • Leverage these ties for deeper academic & professional support
4. Boost FRC Attendance
  • Fall Regional Conference (FRC) is earlier in recruiting cycle than Nationals
  • Allocate budget & plan sign-ups for easier travel, especially if in Texas
  • Many companies will have positions open for members to take advantage of

Soft Goals

These rely on cross-team collaboration or more flexible timelines. Moreso potential ideas that may or not be implemented

1. Increase Cross-Org Socials
  • Collaborate with SHPE, PSP, or other orgs for at least 1 big joint social/hackathon/Lunch and Learn per semester
  • Include Huston-Tillotson (HT) for collab GBMs or socials
  • Foster unity among underrepresented groups in engineering
2. Grow NSBE’s General Career Development
  • Monthly Workshop Series: Cover topics like resume building, personal branding, LinkedIn optimization, and behavioral interview prep.
  • Peer & Alumni Panel Discussions: Invite fellow members or recent grads to share their career trajsectories and tips, ensuring perspectives from various majors.
  • Mock Interview Days: Host practice interviews with upperclassmen, alumni, or company reps to help members gain confidence and sharpen responses.
  • Centralized Resource Hub: Create an easily accessible folder or Slack channel linking to scholarship databases, job boards, and relevant career prep material.

Overarching Goals

Chapter-wide targets that define our broader vision:

1. Win National Large Chapter of the Year
  • Focus on robust membership engagement, impactful events, & consistent corporate partnerships
  • Increase conference attendance (FRC, Nationals)
  • Demonstrate strong community outreach & strategic initiatives that align with NSBE’s mission
2. Propel Members to Proactively Shape Their Careers
  • Introduce a monthly "Member Spotlight" at GBMs, where one student shares a success
  • Highlight members achievements and accomplishments online and expand our LinkedIn presence.
3. Elevate Local Impact & Collaboration
  • Establish deeper ties with local schools (NSBE Jr.) or nearby institutions (Huston-Tillotson) for combined STEM events
  • Empower members to lead service projects or tutoring programs in Austin’s communities
  • Actively track and measure our collective volunteer hours & community impact each semester

Zone-by-Zone Roadmap

Each zone plays a crucial role in bringing our broader goals to life. Below is how each zone can align with specific objectives—like securing corporate partnerships, engaging new and returning members, and growing our chapter’s presence and opportunities.

Membership Zone
  • Promote “Member of the Month” & Spotlights: Work with Communication Zone to highlight top-engaged members or standout accomplishments in newsletters/Instagram posts, reinforcing a culture of recognition.
  • Streamline Point System Data: Collect event check-ins and feed that data to Finance Zone (for merch/gift card rewards)
  • Frequent Feedback Touchpoints: Conduct brief post-event polls or Slack Q&As so members can share immediate impressions, shaping future programming decisions.
Programs Zone
  • Coordinate Corporate & Faculty Events: Ensure each GBM with a corporate guest or Black faculty speaker is well-structured, tying into the goal of having a company at every GBM and inviting approachable faculty.
  • Plan Cross-Org Collaborations: Organize at least one joint social/hackathon/lunch-and-learn per semester with orgs like SHPE or PSP, aligning with your vision for broader underrepresented group unity.
  • Idea Incubator: Poll membership regularly for fresh event suggestions or potential external partnerships ensuring programs stay relevant and engaging.
Finance Zone
  • Manage Sponsor Portal & Budget: Collaborate with the Communication Zone to build out and maintain the “Sponsor Portal” on the official NSBE website, outlining updated packages and sponsor ROI.
  • Track Corporate Luncheons & GBM Costs: Monitor expenses for each corporate-sponsored lunch and assess cost/benefit for expansions.
  • Transparent Expense Reports: Periodically publish a high-level summary of income/expenses so companies know where sponsor dollars and fundraising proceeds are going.
  • Nationals Funding Criteria: Work with Membership to establish a set minimum professional development participation requirement (e.g., attending a set number of workshops or info sessions) for members traveling throught the chapter to Nationals. Similar to like how UH does it
Communication Zone
  • Publicize Upcoming Corporate GBMs: Create Instagram posts and LinkedIn updates spotlighting the specific company attending the next GBM (instead of generic “GBM this Tuesday), reinforcing corporate ties and possibly also boosting engagement from members.
  • Sponsor Portal Promotion: Collaborate with Finance Zone to design and keep the “Sponsor Portal” updated on NSBE’s website, showcasing partner benefits and ways to engage with the chapter.
  • Event Recaps & Engagement Stories: Increase the number of highlight reels and videos going out through the organizations instagram.

Opportunity Initiative & Engagement

One of the biggest hurdles I’ve observed is a lack of applying or taking the initiative to seize the many scholarships, internships, and research openings available. Often, members underestimate how far direct outreach, repeated applications, and leveraging our NSBE network can take them. This is how my recruiting cycle ended up going.

1. “Application Accountability” Events
  • Host monthly “Application Power Hours” where members bring laptops & actively apply to internships, REUs, or scholarships on the spot
  • Upperclassmen or past internship holders rotate around to help tweak resumes, personal statements, or cover letters
  • I've noticed that sometimes you just have to make people take action or else they won't do it
  • Early in each semester, compile an updated list of national NSBE scholarships, REU deadlines, and other grants
  • During the power hours have people review essays or cover letters for those scholarships
2. Emphasizing NSBE’s Community & Support
  • Dedicate time each GBM to highlight a helpful resource—like a Slack “Homework Help” channel or a “Mock Interview” or even create dedicated events for these
  • Share short personal anecdotes of how the NSBE network helped me & others land roles or solve tough classwork
  • Encourage a culture of reciprocal mentoring—everyone invests in each other’s success
3. Making GBMs More Rewarding
  • Raffles & Mystery Prizes at General Body Meetings for small items or gift cards
  • Spotlight opportunities at each GBM: a new scholarship, hackathon, jobs, or upcoming professor’s lab opening. Slack can be ineffective when it comes to communication a lot of the time.
  • Emphasize how crucial it is for members to make the first move when it comes to talking to companies that come and sponsor our events

Implementation & Accountability

Every goal is only as good as our ability to execute & measure it. Here’s how I’ll keep us on track & transparent:

1. Regular Updates towards Goals
  • Short presentation each GBM summarizing progress on current goals
  • Include sponsor updates and the upcoming corporate sponsor for the next GBM
  • Open Q&A for member concerns or new ideas after GBMs with Top 5
2. Semesterly Online Feedback Form
  • An anonymous form at mid-semester to gauge membership satisfaction
  • Collect feedback on events, leadership, or improvement areas
  • Adjust next semester’s approach based on real data & suggestions
3. Officer Accountability
  • Each officer submits a brief “progress report” before E-board meetings
  • Track zone-based deliverables so tasks don’t stall
  • Empower members to see leadership performance & hold them to commitments
  • Fair enforcement: if an officer consistently underperforms, then actions need to be taken to fix the issue
  • A set and enforced strike system in the case of a lack of performance/causing problems
4. CEB Engagement & Meeting Restructure
  • “Talk to 2 new members each GBM” rule: each CEB officer must greet or chat with at least two new or shy members
  • Think Tank Approach: part of each CEB meeting for brainstorming solutions, not just status updates
  • Monthly CEB Socials: off-campus or fun events so the team bonds outside formal tasks
  • Potentially use Miro boards or rotating group tasks if beneficial
  • Don’t just stick to what’s always been done if we can innovate or pivot for better results
